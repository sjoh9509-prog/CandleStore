Our Story

Born in a small kitchen. Grown with intention.

How it began

From Kitchen Table to Candlemaker

Lumière started in 2019 when founder Elise Moreau couldn't find a candle that smelled the way she remembered her grandmother's home in Lyon — warm, herbal, alive. So she started making her own.

What began as a hobby for friends became a small business, then a craft, then a calling. Every candle Lumière makes today is still hand-poured in small batches in Portland, Oregon, using the same unhurried process Elise developed at that kitchen table.

We believe a candle is more than ambiance. It's a ritual. A moment of pause in a world that rarely stops moving.

What we believe

Our Values

01

Slow craft

We never rush a pour. Our candles are made in batches of 24, cured for 72 hours, and inspected by hand before they leave the studio.

02

Clean ingredients

No paraffin, no synthetic dyes, no phthalates. Every ingredient is disclosed because you deserve to know what's burning in your home.

03

Less waste

Our ceramic vessels are designed to outlast the candle. Our packaging uses recycled materials, zero plastic, and soy-based inks.

04

Community first

We source fragrance from small family farms, collaborate with local artists for seasonal labels, and donate 2% of sales to reforestation efforts.

The process

How Every Candle is Made

1

Source

We select each fragrance by nose, not by catalog. Our scents are composed from single-origin oils and botanicals.

2

Blend

The wax is gently melted and blended with fragrance at precise temperatures to ensure even scent distribution through the full burn.

3

Pour

Each candle is poured by hand into its vessel, with wicks centered and secured. No machines, no shortcuts.

4

Cure & Ship

Candles rest for 72 hours before being inspected, labeled by hand, and shipped directly to you.
