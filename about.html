How it began

From Seoul to Portland, One Scent at a Time

Inhwa began in 2020 when founder Soyeon Park moved from Seoul to Portland and realized something was missing — not a person, not a place, but a feeling. The particular smell of her halmoni's apartment in Mapo-gu: mugwort drying in the courtyard, barley tea steeping on the stove, pine resin drifting in through cracked winter windows.

She started blending. First for herself, then for friends who felt the same quiet ache of being far from home. What grew from that kitchen table was Inhwa — which means "beautiful harmony" in Korean — a brand built around the idea that scent is memory's most honest language.

Every candle we make is rooted in a real place, a real season, a real ritual from Korean life. We want you to feel somewhere when you light one.

What we believe

Our Values

01

정 Jeong — Deep connection

Jeong is the untranslatable bond that forms slowly, through time and presence. We make candles the same way: unhurried, in small batches, by hand.

02

Clean ingredients

No paraffin, no synthetic dyes, no phthalates. Every botanical and fragrance oil is disclosed because you deserve to know what's burning in your home.

03

눈치 Nunchi — Mindful presence

Nunchi is the Korean art of quiet awareness. Our vessels are designed to remain in your space long after the candle is gone — as planters, brush holders, tea cups.

04

뿌리 Ppuri — Staying rooted

Ppuri means roots. We source Korean botanicals from family farms in Jeju and Gyeonggi, and donate 2% of sales to Korean cultural preservation programs.

The process

How Every Candle is Made

1

Source

We select each fragrance by nose, inspired by specific Korean places and seasons. Our botanicals — omija, ssuk, chrysanthemum, Jeju tangerine zest — are sourced from small farms we trust.

2

Blend

The coconut-soy wax is gently melted and blended with fragrance at precise temperatures, following a ritual refined over hundreds of test pours. Never rushed.

3

Pour

Each candle is hand-poured into its celadon-inspired ceramic vessel, wicks centered and held by hand until set. No machines. No shortcuts.

4

Cure & Ship

Candles rest for 72 hours — we call it the "resting room," like the quiet before a jesa ceremony. Then each jar is inspected, labeled by hand, and wrapped for its journey to you.
